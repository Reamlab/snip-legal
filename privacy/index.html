Privacy Policy for Snip

Last updated: June 30, 2026 Effective date: June 30, 2026

I'm Ilya Razuvaev, an independent developer, and this is how the Snip app for macOS handles your data. Snip has no account and no login. Read this together with the Terms of Use.

1. Your Images Stay on Your Device

Everything Snip does to your pictures — background removal, object erase, upscaling/enhancement, screen capture, and text recognition (OCR) — runs locally on your Mac using Apple's on-device frameworks (CoreML and Vision).

The app does not upload or transmit your images, your screenshots, or the text it recognizes. That content never leaves your device and is never sent to me or any third party.

Two limited kinds of data do leave your device: the purchase data needed to manage in-app purchases (Section 4), and usage analytics that help me improve and stabilize the app (Section 5). Neither is tied to your name or email, and neither ever includes your images, screenshots, or recognized text.

2. Who I Am (Data Controller)

For the GDPR and similar laws, the controller is Ilya Razuvaev, an independent developer (no company). Contact: snipfeedback@gmail.com. Questions about your data go to that email.

3. What I Collect

  • Purchase and subscription data — only if you buy something, handled through Apple and the subscription service Apphud (Section 4).
  • Usage analytics — which features you use, diagnostic/error events, app version, OS version, device model, and a device-scoped identifier (Section 5).

I do not collect your name, email, account or login data, precise location, contacts, microphone, or camera input. I do not collect an advertising identifier (IDFA), and I do not do cross-app or cross-site tracking. I do not sell or "share" your personal information, and I do not use your content to train any models.

4. Purchases (Apple and Apphud)

If you buy a subscription (Monthly or Annual) or the one-time "Lifetime" purchase, Apple processes the transaction through the App Store. To manage purchases, validate receipts, restore them, and apply the right entitlement (free vs. paid), Snip uses Apphud, Inc. for subscription management.

Apphud receives a device identifier (Apple's Identifier for Vendor, IDFV — not a hardware serial or advertising ID) and purchase details from Apple (which product, transaction/receipt info, subscription status, renewal/expiration, trial status, storefront). Apphud does not receive any of your images, screenshots, or recognized text.

Apple handles your payment, billing, and App Store account as an independent controller under Apple's own privacy policy.

5. Analytics (Amplitude and Firebase)

Snip's analytics is on for everyone and helps me see how features are used and keep the app stable. It uses Amplitude, Inc. (US) and Google Firebase Analytics (Google LLC, US).

These services collect:

  • usage events (which tools and features you use, sessions, feature flows, taps);
  • diagnostic and error events;
  • app version, OS version, device model;
  • a device-scoped identifier (Apple's IDFV and/or a Firebase app-instance ID) used to count distinct devices and group events from the same device; and
  • your IP address (received by Firebase).

This identifier is not your name or email, is not an advertising ID, and is not linked to your identity. The analytics describes how the app is used, not what you process with it: your images, screenshots, and recognized text are never included and stay on your device.

Legal basis (GDPR). I rely on my legitimate interests under Art. 6(1)(f) — improving and stabilizing the app — which I consider proportionate because the data is device-scoped, contains no image content, and is not used for advertising or profiling.

Your choices. There is currently no in-app toggle to turn analytics off. You can email me at snipfeedback@gmail.com to ask about your options.

6. Data Stored Only on Your Device

These never leave your Mac:

  • Free-usage counter (e.g. the daily free-tier limit), in the macOS Keychain.
  • History of recent edits and results, in your local Application Support folder.
  • Preferences and settings, in UserDefaults.

Delete the app (and its data) or clear history in the app to remove this.

7. Processors and International Transfers

Processor Purpose Data received
Apphud, Inc. (US) Subscription management, receipt validation Device identifier (IDFV), purchase/subscription data
Amplitude, Inc. (US) Product analytics Usage and diagnostic events, app/device info, device-scoped identifier

Apple separately handles payment, billing, and your App Store account as an independent controller.

International transfers. These providers may process data in the United States. Amplitude and Google both certify under the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework (with the UK Extension and the Swiss–U.S. DPF) and use the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as a fallback; Amplitude also offers EU data residency. Apphud and Apple rely on SCCs or other lawful transfer mechanisms. Email me for more detail.

8. Data Retention

  • Images, screenshots, recognized text: never received by me, so never retained by me. Local copies stay on your device until you delete them.
  • On-device data (counter, history, preferences): until you delete it or uninstall.
  • Purchase data: kept by Apple and Apphud as needed to manage your subscription and meet legal/tax obligations.
  • Analytics: kept by Amplitude and Google under their own retention limits, then deleted or anonymized.

9. Your Rights

GDPR / UK GDPR. You can request access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection (including to the analytics covered by Section 5), and data portability, and you can lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. Email snipfeedback@gmail.com. Because there's no account and the analytics is not tied to your identity, I may be unable to locate your specific data without more information from you, and some requests may need to go to Apple, Apphud, Amplitude, or Google — I'll help where I can.

California (CCPA/CPRA) and other US states. I do not sell or "share" personal information and have not done so in the past 12 months; analytics is first-party and not used for advertising. Categories that may be collected: identifiers (device-scoped ID), commercial information (purchase records), and internet/network activity (usage and diagnostic events). You have the right to know, delete, correct, and to be free from discrimination for exercising these rights. Contact snipfeedback@gmail.com. Because I don't sell, share, profile, or do targeted advertising, the related opt-outs under state laws don't apply to my processing.

10. Security

Your images never leave your Mac, so the main protection for them is the security of your own device. For the limited purchase data, I rely on Apple's and Apphud's safeguards, including encryption in transit. No system is perfectly secure, but Snip's on-device design keeps the data exposed to a minimum.

11. Children

Snip is for general audiences and is not directed to children under 13 (or the higher digital-consent age in your country). I do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided data, email snipfeedback@gmail.com and I'll delete it.

12. Changes

I may update this policy. The current version lives at https://reamlab.github.io/snip-legal/privacy/ with an updated "Last updated" date. For material changes I'll take reasonable steps to notify you and, where required, get your consent first.
